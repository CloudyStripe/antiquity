# Antiquity — Course Map (Curriculum Blueprint)

This is the steerable plan for the whole curriculum. **Only the Foundations era is authored as playable content in v0.1** (see `content/curriculum.json`); everything below it is on the map as "planned" teasers so you can see — and reshape — the full arc before we build it out. Reorder, cut, split, or add anything.

**Design rules baked into this map**
- *Modular & bite-size, never shallow.* Each unit is a 5–10 min module; **depth lives in separate, optional deep-dive modules (◇)** — never inside a unit. A deep topic can spawn several deep-dive modules, which unlock once their parent unit is complete.
- *Comprehensive but interleaved — no binge, no snapshots.* Every civilization is covered in full, **rise to fall** — never a single drive-by snapshot, and always entered at its *beginning*. But we don't march one civ start-to-finish in a single block (that's burnout). Each era moves in chronological **rounds** — a little Mesopotamia, then Egypt, then Minoan, then back — so you always feel *"meanwhile, elsewhere…"*. Each module is badged with its civilization, and you can optionally follow one civ's arc on its own.
- *"How We Know" in every unit.* A recurring evidence panel: the actual data behind a claim, and how confident we should be (`established` / `contested` / `open`).
- *Open-minded with integrity.* Where there's real debate, a "Debate" unit presents mainstream + serious alternative(s) + the evidence and weaknesses of each — no side pre-judged.
- *The Americas are first-class*, woven throughout (marked ✦), not bolted on.
- ✦ = Americas focus · ◆ = Mystery / Open-Question unit · ◇ = optional deep-dive module.

## ERA 0 — Foundations: How to Think About Ancient History  ✅ BUILDING NOW (6 core units + deep dives)

The toolkit. You can't judge a date or a mystery without knowing how dates and claims are made.

1. **What Counts as "Ancient History"?** — prehistory vs. history (writing as the conventional line), the deep-time problem, BCE/CE and "c." conventions, why "civilization" is a loaded word.
2. **The Three-Age System: Stone, Bronze, Iron** — how Thomsen invented it sorting a museum (1836), why it's a *framework, not a law of nature*, and where it breaks down across regions.
3. **Stone Age Lifeways: Paleo / Meso / Neolithic** — what the "-lithic" terms actually mean (chipped vs. ground stone; foraging vs. farming), who coined them, the Neolithic Revolution, and the crucial catch: these boundaries happen at *different times in different places*.
4. **How We Know I — Stratigraphy & Relative Dating** — superposition, context/association, seriation. Why *where* a thing was found matters more than the thing.
5. **How We Know II — Radiocarbon & Absolute Dating** — Libby's clock, half-life, what can be dated, why radiocarbon years ≠ calendar years, calibration with tree-rings, and error bars.
6. **How We Know III — When Experts Disagree** ◆ — consensus vs. minority view vs. fringe; what makes a claim testable; how to weigh competing explanations fairly.

**Optional deep-dive modules** (◇ — unlock after their parent unit; skip freely, they never block progress):
- ◇ **Why the Three-Age System Breaks Down in the Americas** — extends Unit 2. No true "Bronze Age" across much of the New World — so how *do* we periodize it? *(authored in v0.1)*
- ◇ **The Tree-Ring Clock: How Calibration Works** — extends Unit 5. Dendrochronology, and how it converts radiocarbon years into calendar years. *(authored in v0.1)*
- ◇ **Case File: The Age of the Sphinx** — extends Unit 6. The water-erosion debate in full — Khafre vs. a far older core — weighed position by position. *(authored in v0.1)*
- ◇ **When Radiocarbon Misleads** — extends Unit 5. Old-wood, contamination, and marine-reservoir effects. *(planned)*
- ◇ **Case File: The Stones of Sacsayhuamán** — extends Unit 6. Wonder, demonstrated method, and where fringe claims fall short. *(planned)*

## ERA 1 — Before Cities: The Deep Past & First Monuments  ✅ PLAYABLE (7 units)

The shared backstory of every civilization that follows (developments, not yet civilizations).

- **Out of Africa** — tracing the human journey across the world (stone tools + ancient DNA).
- **The Symbolic Mind** — cave art from Chauvet to Lascaux; how you date a painting.
- **Göbekli Tepe** ◆ — monumental ritual architecture *before* farming? The site that scrambled the "farming-first" story.
- **The Neolithic Revolution & the First Towns** — farming begins (independently, in several places); Çatalhöyük, a town with no streets.
- **Megaliths of Atlantic Europe** — Stonehenge across its building phases; how you date a standing stone.
- **The Younger Dryas & the Impact Hypothesis** ◆ — did a cosmic event reshape the climate, and our story? Evidence and critics.
- **Peopling of the Americas** ✦ ◆ — how "Clovis-first" was overturned (Monte Verde, the White Sands footprints): a paradigm shift, shown as it happened.

## ERA 2 — The Bronze Age World  *(Round 1 ✅ playable · rest planned · ~3500–1150 BCE)*

*Opens with a parallel timeline. Taught in interleaved rounds — you hop between civilizations as they rise, peak, and fall, so you always feel what's happening elsewhere at the same moment. Each civ is still followed to its end (marked **arc complete**).*

**Round 1 — The Dawn of Cities (~3500–2900 BCE)** ✅ playable
- **Mesopotamia** — Ubaid & Uruk: the first cities and the first writing (cuneiform).
- **Egypt** — the Predynastic Nile → unification under Narmer.
- ✦ **Norte Chico / Caral** — monumental cities rising on the Peruvian coast, as old as anything in the Old World.

**Round 2 — The First Kingdoms (~2900–2300 BCE)**
- **Mesopotamia** — the Early Dynastic city-states (Ur, Lagash) and their wars.
- **Egypt** — the Old Kingdom and the Pyramid Age; ◆ the Great Sphinx (who built it, and when?).
- **Indus** — the Early Harappan build-up.
- **Minoan Crete** — Early Minoan beginnings (the Prepalatial world; deep roots back to Neolithic Knossos).
- ✦ **Caral** — at its height.

**Round 3 — The First Empires (~2300–1800 BCE)**
- **Mesopotamia** — Sargon's Akkadian Empire (the first empire) → its fall → the Ur III revival.
- **Egypt** — the First Intermediate collapse → the Middle Kingdom's golden age.
- **Indus** — Mature Harappan peak: Mohenjo-daro, planned cities, ◆ the undeciphered script.
- **Minoan Crete** — the Old Palaces (Protopalatial); Linear A appears.
- ✦ **Caral** — decline (~1800): **arc complete.**

**Round 4 — The International Age (~1800–1300 BCE)**
- **Mesopotamia** — Hammurabi's Babylon and its laws → the Hittite sack of Babylon (1595): **Bronze-Age arc closes** (the region returns in Era 3).
- **Egypt** — the Hyksos (Second Intermediate) → the New Kingdom rises (Hatshepsut, Thutmose).
- **Minoan Crete** — the New Palaces, the Minoan peak; ◆ the Thera eruption and its dating dispute (a direct callback to the Foundations dating units).
- **Hittites** — rise to empire in Anatolia.
- **Mycenaean Greece** — rise (the shaft graves) toward palatial power.
- **Indus** — decline (~1900): **arc complete.**

**Round 5 — Zenith & Collapse (~1300–1150 BCE)**
- **Egypt** — the New Kingdom's height: Akhenaten's revolution, Tutankhamun, Ramesses, Kadesh (Egypt continues, weakened, into Era 3).
- **Hittites** — Kadesh and the first recorded peace treaty → collapse (~1180): **arc complete.**
- **Mycenaean Greece** — the Linear B palaces (a decipherment triumph) → collapse: **arc complete.**
- **Minoan Crete** — Mycenaean takeover → final fall: **arc complete.**
- ◆ **The Bronze Age Collapse** — the shared ending: the Sea Peoples, drought, systems failure.

## ERA 3 — The Iron Age & First Empires  *(planned · ~1150–500 BCE)*

*Interleaved rounds again. Iron, the alphabet, and empires of millions.*

**Round 1 — Out of the Dark (~1150–900 BCE)**
- **Egypt** — the Third Intermediate Period (fragmentation; Libyan dynasties).
- **Phoenicians** — the city-states rise; the **alphabet** takes shape; trade fans across the Mediterranean.
- **Israel & Judah** — emergence and the (debated) united monarchy: archaeology *and* text.
- ✦ **Mesoamerica** — the first Olmec stirrings.

**Round 2 — The Age of Assyria (~900–610 BCE)**
- **Assyria** — the Neo-Assyrian Empire: the first machine of empire → Ashurbanipal's library → sudden fall (Nineveh, 612): **arc complete.**
- **Kush / Nubia** ✦-adjacent (Africa) — rise, and the 25th Dynasty **"Black Pharaohs"** who rule Egypt.
- **Phoenicians** — Carthage founded; colonies across the sea.
- ✦ **Olmec** — San Lorenzo & La Venta, the colossal heads ◆.
- **Israel** — the Assyrian conquest of the northern kingdom (722).

**Round 3 — New Powers (~610–500 BCE)**
- **Babylon** — the Neo-Babylonian Empire: Nebuchadnezzar, the exile of Judah, ◆ the Hanging Gardens → fall to Persia (539): **arc complete.**
- **Egypt** — the Late Period (Saite revival) under mounting pressure.
- **Kush** — the move south to Meroë (and its own script).
- ✦ **Chavín de Huántar** — the Andean religious horizon spreads.
- ✦ **Olmec** — decline (~400 BCE): **arc complete.**

## ERA 4 — Classical Worlds  *(planned · ~550 BCE–)*

*Interleaved rounds. World empires, scripts we can read, and the Americas at full height.*

**Round 1 — The First World Empire (~550–330 BCE)**
- **Persia** — Cyrus founds the Achaemenid Empire; the Royal Road; ◇ Behistun, the key that cracked cuneiform.
- **Greece** — the Archaic polis → Classical Athens, democracy, the Greco-Persian and Peloponnesian wars.
- ✦ **Maya** — the Preclassic rise; the first cities and the Long Count taking shape.
- **Rome** — the early Republic finding its feet.

**Round 2 — Conquerors (~330–150 BCE)**
- **Greece / Macedon** — Alexander topples Persia (**Persia's arc completes**) → the Hellenistic world.
- **Maurya India** — Chandragupta → **Ashoka**, whose rock edicts are the primary source.
- **China** — the Warring States → **Qin** unification (the terracotta army ◆) → the early Han.
- **Rome** — expansion; the Punic Wars against Carthage.
- ✦ **Teotihuacan** — begins its rise.

**Round 3 — Empires at the Turn of the Era (~150 BCE–250 CE)**
- **Rome** — Republic to Empire; ◆ Baalbek's trilithon (colossal Roman-era megaliths, told straight).
- **Han China** — the height; the **Silk Road** opens (Rome and China dimly aware of each other).
- ✦ **Andes** — the Moche and the Nazca (the lines ◆).
- ✦ **Teotihuacan** — a giant city with no obvious king ◆.
- ✦ **Maya** — into the Classic period.

**Round 4 — Heights & Falls (~250–900 CE)**
- ✦ **Maya** — the Classic peak: a readable script, the Long Count, the great city-states → the "collapse," reconsidered.
- ✦ **Teotihuacan** — burning and fall (~550): **arc complete.**
- ✦ **Andes** — Tiwanaku & Wari → the road to the Inca, with ◇ **Sacsayhuamán's masonry**: wonder, demonstrated method, and where fringe claims fall short.
- **Rome & Han** — each meets its own end (hooks to wherever we draw the finish line).
